Vulnerability Disclosure Policy (VDP)

Last Updated: November 15, 2025

Introduction

The security of my projects and systems is a top priority. I value the work of the security community and encourage the responsible disclosure of any potential vulnerabilities. This Vulnerability Disclosure Policy (VDP) outlines how to submit reports, what to expect from the process, and the guidelines that must be followed.

Scope

This policy applies to security vulnerabilities found in any of my publicly accessible digital assets. Vulnerabilities found in third-party systems or outside the defined scope are not covered by this policy.

Rules of Engagement

When researching and reporting vulnerabilities, you must adhere to these rules:

  • Do no harm: Avoid actions that could degrade system performance, corrupt data, or disrupt access for other users.
  • Respect privacy: Do not access, modify, or store data that does not belong to you. If you encounter user data, stop your activity and notify me immediately.
  • Act in good faith: Do not engage in denial of service (DoS/DDoS) attacks, spam, social engineering, or phishing.
  • Maintain confidentiality: Do not publicly disclose any information about the vulnerability until a fix has been implemented and I have given explicit consent.

How to Report a Vulnerability

To report a vulnerability, please send a detailed email to jessicaferreirateixeira@gmail.com.

To ensure a swift and effective analysis, your report should include:

  • Descriptive Title: Ex: [VDP] Reflected Cross-Site Scripting (XSS) on [URL]
  • Vulnerability Summary: A clear description of the flaw and its potential impact.
  • Affected Asset: The exact URL, API endpoint, or location where the vulnerability was found.
  • Steps to Reproduce: Detailed instructions that allow me to replicate the issue.
  • Proof of Concept (PoC): Scripts, screenshots, videos, or logs that demonstrate the vulnerability.

My Commitment (SLA)

  • Acknowledgment: I will do my best to acknowledge receipt of your report within 3 business days.
  • Triage and Analysis: My goal is to validate and assess the severity of your report within 7 business days.
  • Transparent Communication: I will keep you informed about the progress of the fix.
  • Recognition: I appreciate and value every contribution. Public recognition can be offered with your consent after the vulnerability is resolved.

Contact

For any questions about this policy or for other security-related matters, please contact me at: jessicaferreirateixeira@gmail.com.
